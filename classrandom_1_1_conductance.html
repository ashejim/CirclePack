double[][] random.Conductance.adjacencyMatrix ( PackData p,
boolean stdt )
static

Save adjacency matrix of packing (<= 10,000 verts) in form for matlab to read; the adjacency matrix has a 1 in (i,j) spot if i and j are neighboring vertices, else a 0.

If 'tm' is true, divide every row by sum of its entries and set the rows for boundary vertices to zero: this yields the "transition" matrix for the "simple" (ie. equal probability) random walk on the edge-graph of the packing with absorbing boundary. Also add an ordered list of the boundary vertices to the matlab file.

double[][] random.Conductance.setConductances ( PackData domData)
static

Compute conductances of the triangulation based on packing centers. If 'domData' is a packing, this is usual set of eucl conductances (a la Dubejko). However, in general, use ratio of distance between incircle centers of bounding faces to length of their common edge. (For bdry edges, use inRadius/edgelength.) (Note: elsewhere, compute transition probability from v to u by dividing edge <v,u> conductance by sum of conductances of all edges from v.).
