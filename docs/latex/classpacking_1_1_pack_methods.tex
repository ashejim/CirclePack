int packing.\+Pack\+Methods.\+write\+Dual\+Barys (\begin{DoxyParamCaption}\item[{Buffered\+Writer}]{fp}{, }\item[{\doxymbox{\hyperlink{classpacking_1_1_pack_data}{Pack\+Data}}}]{p}{, }\item[{\doxymbox{\hyperlink{classpacking_1_1_pack_data}{Pack\+Data}}}]{bp}{}\end{DoxyParamCaption})\hspace{0.3cm}{\ttfamily [static]}}



Specialized routine for sending paths in barycentric coordinates to a file, part of the work with John Bowers for 3D printing software. 

We write the dual graph of \textquotesingle{}p\textquotesingle{} to a file. The path is represented as a chain of face/\+start bary/\+end bary groups representing segments in barycentric not for \textquotesingle{}p\textquotesingle{} itself, but rather for a second packing \textquotesingle{}bp\textquotesingle{} (associated, e.\+g., with an underlying conformal map). Thus we need to find the paths in the dual graph of \textquotesingle{}p\textquotesingle{}, but convert those based on \textquotesingle{}bp\textquotesingle{}. Since the face indices of \textquotesingle{}bp\textquotesingle{} are not persistent, have to indicate faces with triples of vertices.

In .q files, use key word "{}\+BARY\+\_\+\+PATH:\+"{} followed by groups of three lines:\+ \begin{DoxyVerb} v1 v2 v3 
    s1 s2  
    e1 e2 
\end{DoxyVerb}


$<$v1 v2 v3$>$ is face, s\+\_\+j bary coords of start, e\+\_\+j bary coords of end, (third bary coord is computed from first 2). There may be many such segments.
