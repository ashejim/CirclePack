\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Alternate procedure for laying out spherical circle packings:\+ 

Alternate procedure for laying out spherical circle packings:\+ Identify 4 \textquotesingle{}beacons\textquotesingle{}, combinatorially well-\/distributed vertices. For each, puncture and max pack, then record the inv distances of other points to that beacon. Now quadrangulate circles with respect to the beacons.

Points of H\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}3 (hyp 3D) are represented points on one hyperboloid sheet:\+ ~\newline


S+= \{(t,x,y,z):\+ t\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2-\/x\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2-\/y\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2-\/z\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2 = 1, t\texorpdfstring{$>$}{>}0\}

Points of S\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2, the boundary, comprise the Riemann sphere

S\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2=\{(x,y,z):\+ x\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2+y\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2+z\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2 = 1\}

Discs on S\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2 are represented by points on a related one-\/sheeted hyperboloid. Discs are

\{(t,x,y,z):\+ t\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2-\/x\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2-\/y\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2-\/z\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2 = -\/1\}, t\texorpdfstring{$>$}{>}0

Boundary of disc (t,x,y,z) is

\{(a,b,c):\+a\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2+b\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2+c\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2=1, ax+by+cz=t\}

where t/\+(x\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2+y\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2+z\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}2)=cos(r), r = sph radius.

(TODO:\+ Not sure if this gives oriented disc:\+ how about t\texorpdfstring{$<$}{<}0? and great circles seem to be t=0.)

The inversive distance between discs (t,x,y,z) and (s,a,b,c) is st-\/ax-\/by-\/cz.
