\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
A. 

\begin{DoxySeeAlso}{See also}
Gen\+Branch\+Pt of the "{}singular"{} type conceived
\end{DoxySeeAlso}
A \begin{DoxySeeAlso}{See also}
Gen\+Branch\+Pt of the "{}singular"{} type conceived by Ed Crane. The event horizon consists of the three vertices of the specified \textquotesingle{}sing\+Face\textquotesingle{} and the neighboring "{}connection"{} circles (so named by James Ashe). The face index is ephemeral, so depend on the event horizon to build and \doxylink{classbranching_1_1_sing_br_mod_pt_a00b6de1bb496ffa88c1efaabdf51d6f7}{dismantle}. The face vertices 1,2,3 correspond in order with vertices of \textquotesingle{}sing\+Face\textquotesingle{} in the parent. We add a central "{}vanishing"{} circle (with aim 4\texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*}pi) and 3 "{}guide"{} circles between the original vertices. Two overlap angles are the parameters (along with a third so they sum to PI). Each original vertex j shares overlap\+[j]\+ with each of its guide circle neighbors. (Terminology from paper by Ashe, Crane, and Stephenson.)
\end{DoxySeeAlso}
Note:\+ E.\+g., to get overlap ov on edge (2,3) of \textquotesingle{}sing\+Face\textquotesingle{}, we have corner\+[1]\+ overlap nghbs guide\+[2]\+ and guide\+[3]\+ by ov.

Construction goes like this:\+ (1) \textquotesingle{}corner\textquotesingle{}s and \textquotesingle{}face\+Edge\textquotesingle{}s are the circles/\+edges of \textquotesingle{}sing\+Face\textquotesingle{}. (2) \textquotesingle{}vanishing\textquotesingle{} is created as barycenter of \textquotesingle{}sing\+Face\textquotesingle{} (3) each \textquotesingle{}face\+Edge\textquotesingle{} is split, creating a \textquotesingle{}guide\textquotesingle{} vertex and a \textquotesingle{}second\+Edge\textquotesingle{}.

Associations:\+ (Note:\+ indexing will be from 0) (\texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*}) corner\+[j]\+ is the origin of face\+Edge\+[j]\+. (\texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*}) when we split face\+Edge\+[j]\+, the new vertex created is guide\+[(j+2)\%3]\+ (so guide\+[j]\+ is opposite corner\+[j]\+) (\texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*}) after all the splittings, corner\+[j]\+ has two edges from it, face\+Edge\+[j]\+ and also second\+Edge\+[(j+2)\%3]\+. These edges both are assigned cos\+\_\+overs\+[j]\+ as inversive distance.

To dismantle, we meld face\+Edge\+[3]\+/\+\+[2]\+/\+\+[1]\+ (in that order) and the remove the barycenter \textquotesingle{}vanishing\textquotesingle{}.
