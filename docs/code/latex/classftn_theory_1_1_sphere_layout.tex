\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Alternate procedure for laying out spherical circle packings:\+ 

Alternate procedure for laying out spherical circle packings:\+ Identify 4 \textquotesingle{}beacons\textquotesingle{}, combinatorially well-\/distributed vertices. For each, puncture and max pack, then record the inv distances of other points to that beacon. Now quadrangulate circles with respect to the beacons.

Points of H$^\wedge$3 (hyp 3D) are represented points on one hyperboloid sheet:\+ ~\newline


S+= \{(t,x,y,z):\+ t$^\wedge$2-\/x$^\wedge$2-\/y$^\wedge$2-\/z$^\wedge$2 = 1, t$>$0\}

Points of S$^\wedge$2, the boundary, comprise the Riemann sphere

S$^\wedge$2=\{(x,y,z):\+ x$^\wedge$2+y$^\wedge$2+z$^\wedge$2 = 1\}

Discs on S$^\wedge$2 are represented by points on a related one-\/sheeted hyperboloid. Discs are

\{(t,x,y,z):\+ t$^\wedge$2-\/x$^\wedge$2-\/y$^\wedge$2-\/z$^\wedge$2 = -\/1\}, t$>$0

Boundary of disc (t,x,y,z) is

\{(a,b,c):\+a$^\wedge$2+b$^\wedge$2+c$^\wedge$2=1, ax+by+cz=t\}

where t/\+(x$^\wedge$2+y$^\wedge$2+z$^\wedge$2)=cos(r), r = sph radius.

(TODO:\+ Not sure if this gives oriented disc:\+ how about t$<$0? and great circles seem to be t=0.)

The inversive distance between discs (t,x,y,z) and (s,a,b,c) is st-\/ax-\/by-\/cz.
