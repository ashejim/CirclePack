\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Describes a graph with nodes in linked list of \textquotesingle{}\doxyref{Edge\+Simple}{p.}{classkomplex_1_1_edge_simple}\textquotesingle{}s. 

Describes a graph with nodes in linked list of \textquotesingle{}\doxyref{Edge\+Simple}{p.}{classkomplex_1_1_edge_simple}\textquotesingle{}s. Nodes indexed by natural numbers (zero not included, though 0 can be used to denote a root) and we must keep track of maximum node index. Our graphs do not allow multiple edges or loops (i.\+e., of form $<$f,f$>$). Examples of use are graphs and dual graphs of circle packings.

Acyclic graphs have no closed edge-\/paths. Tree\textquotesingle{}s are special case:\+ only a tree/\+forest (union of disjoint trees) can have \textquotesingle{}roots\textquotesingle{}, which are pairs of the form $<$0,f$>$. A tree/\+forest is not in proper form until each tree has "{}directed"{} edges (orientation $<$g,f$>$ from g to f) from a unique root. \begin{DoxyAuthor}{Author}
